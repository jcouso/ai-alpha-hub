## 🔴 Top Signals

### 1. Anthropic: "When AI Builds Itself" — 80% of Code Now Written by Claude
Anthropic published a landmark report titled "When AI Builds Itself" (June 4). Key data: >80% of code merged into Anthropic's production codebase in May 2026 was authored by Claude — up from low single digits before Claude Code's Feb 2025 preview. Engineers now ship ~8x as much code per day as in 2024. The report explicitly calls for a verifiable, internationally coordinated "pause button" for frontier AI development, modeled on nuclear arms control frameworks. This is the first major AI lab publicly acknowledging recursive self-improvement as an active concern, not a theoretical one.
- **Source:** [Anthropic Institute](https://www.anthropic.com/institute/recursive-self-improvement) / [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-says-80-of-its-new-production-code-is-now-authored-by-claude-how-your-enterprise-can-keep-up) / [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/anthropic-says-claude-now-writes-over-90-of-its-code-and-wants-the-world-to-have-an-ai-pause-button/) / [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/anthropic-says-claude-now-writes-more-than-80-percent-of-its-merged-code)
- **Verification:** CONFIRMED (official Anthropic blog)
- **Why it matters:** This is the clearest evidence yet that AI-writing-AI is not future speculation — it's production reality at the frontier lab itself. For technical founders: the productivity multiplier is real, but so is the dependency risk.

### 3. US House Unveils Bipartisan AI Bill — Would Preempt State Laws for 3 Years
Reps. Obernolte (R-CA) and Trahan (D-MA) released a long-awaited draft of national AI legislation (June 4). Key provision: a 3-year preemption of state laws regulating AI model development. Also requires top developers to disclose safety and security risks of new models.
- **Source:** [POLITICO](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/06/04/obernolte-trahan-ai-bill-lands-on-the-hill-00949920) / [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/us-house-lawmakers-release-draft-bill-regulate-ai-2026-06-04/) / [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/04/house-draft-bill-regulate-ai)
- **Verification:** CONFIRMED (Congressional draft released)
- **Why it matters:** If passed, this simplifies the regulatory landscape dramatically — one federal framework instead of a state-by-state patchwork. Huge for AI startups selling nationally.
